# No Custody of Funds

There is no entity that custodies or can recover lost user funds. User funds are held directly on the blockchain using public-private key signing. All users MUST assure they have backed up their seed phrase (12 words). Recovery from seed is the ONLY way users can access their wallet.

# No Warranty

Vox comes with no promised or implied warranty. Software is provided "as-is". Users interact with the blockchain directly via public index servers. There are no "services" provided, only software that allows users to interact with contracts and protocols. The app reads data from the blockchain, does calculations to attempt to express user intent, then allows users to post new data to the blockchain. 

Users are reading the blockchain, doing calculations with their computers and broadcasting new transactions to servers maintaining the network. 

## Commodities and Futures

Vox may provide users with exposure to tokens with fair issuance that all people have and equal opportunity to procure and bring to market. These assets are colloquially called *commodities*. Vox provides users with exposure to regular series of tokens intended to represent a corresponding amount of coin at some future date, i.e. *futures*.

However, regulatory authorities may not have the agency to speak plainly regarding the classification of such assets on a blockchain their owners do not control. 

## No Vox-issued Securities

Vox will NEVER issue shares or securities. Vox is not associated with any ICOs or privately issued scrip currencies or meme coins. 

Users MAY dump any junk asset they find in their wallet via dutch auction (or likewise obtain junk securities for speculation at auction). 

## No Swaps

Vox provides NO exposure to fiat currencies or so-called "stablecoins". There are NO vox apps that allow users to enter into a contract with a counterparty based on the fiat price set by an oracle. 

Vox has no native app support for apps where users stake coins for tokens based on the fiat currency price provided by and oracle. 

# Privacy

Vox mini-apps use a suite of public contracts that may be interacted with pseudonymously. Any user seeking the optimal gain on most of their liquid coins will repeatedly join (and therefore associate) individual "anonymous" outputs into one. Associating many coins together in a currency like bitcoin is a strong indication the total output belongs to the same user.

Neither CashFusion, nor Hierarchical Deterministic (HD) technology will preserve user privacy interacting regularly and freely with a group of public contracts. For this reason, and for simplicity, Vox just uses a single address (like and Ethereum style wallet). 

Vox is NOT recommended as a daily wallet for in-person or retail transactions.

As a finance application suite, free people are allowed to engage in free trade and hold sound money. They do not need to hide, feel shame nor fear having a nominal amount of agency. 

## Vox Pop is a permanent archive

Part of the goal of the Vox chat app is to create a permanent readable archive that can be indexed and searched later. Although posts may be removed early from the channel, their existence as transactions can never be erased or entirely censored. That is a feature.
