## Why give to VOX•CASH?

VOX•CASH is supported by members of the Bitcoin Cash community. 

Vox.cash is a testbed of new bitcoin contracts, protocols, and markets. It's a continuation of ideas started with [unspent.app](https://unspent.app) and [futurebitcoin.cash](https://futurebitcoin.cash), but enabled with an integrated wallet and more advanced contract template system (libauth).

### Generic contracts can benefit everyone

Everyone benefits from a suite of simple standard contracts.

But with neutral general purpose contracts, it's not trivial for one party to get all the coins, so although everyone can benefit from these contracts existing, there aren't big investors looking to finance and promote them―quite the opposite. Every coin that is locked in a sound protocol is money that can't be easily be taken with a contract biased toward user liquidation.

General purpose protocols are cheap to develop and live forever. Many of the mini-app contracts have self-financing mechanisms to allow miners (or anyone) to benefit from maintaining the contract instances. Anyone with access to the Bitcoin Cash blockchain can benefit from these protocols.

Vox is promoted by word of mouth. It is also funded the annuities of earlier fundraisers. Ultimately, having many users who all benefit is the best promotion strategy. 

### Vox can be *your* zen garden

The vox app and protocols are NOT funded by user liquidations. 

There is no pathway in any of the protocols to liquidate users based on oracle prices. The protocols are specifically designed to be resistant to undue influence from a well-capitalized actor who can set oracles. 

There are no mysterious investors or liquidity providers bankrolling development and pumping the total locked value. The apps developed here aren't likely to be mentioned favorably in the well-financed Bitcoin Cash Spaces or Podcasts.

In such a context, both Vox (and earlier iterations) have been the subject of targeted libel campaigns by bad actors in the Bitcoin Cash community who engage in paid trolling under the guise of mental illness. At the same time, prominent leaders in Bitcoin Cash, acting as moderators, watch as this project and stream of new developers with fresh ideas are relentlessly trolled in public spaces away from a community with entrenched toxic interests. They allow bad actors and bad behavior to persist because it protects their income (and investors) from fair competition. 

But protocols are resistant to libel and sabotage. Bitcoin Script won't give up if someone maligns it or if a fundraiser fails. Code written on bitcoin doesn't care what people on the internet said. 

But if obvious trolls were allowed to act as gatekeepers to general spaces, and all new developer talent were funneled to the oracle liquidation app career pipeline, then as a bitcoin project, we would be pretty cooked.

As a response to the current situation of public spaces in Bitcoin Cash, Vox channels are spaces that can't be captured or cheaply moderated by bad actors. 

Vox channels are way for everyone with Bitcoin Cash to have a say, and where people who refuse to use peer-to-peer currency will naturally self-censor. 

Vox chats are spaces where trolling and bad behavior are on the record. The attack/retract tactic has a trail. 

Bucketshop shills can have the biggest telegram groups. They can have the most subscribers and the most followers. They can be the moderators of the major subreddits. Engagement is a cheap commodity on centralized platforms. They will keep building cheap off-chain copies of vox to shill.

Oracle-pegged liquidation protocols can have the largest total locked values on launch day. And swap contracts will get instantly reloaded after every major liquidation event to keep the top spot. 

Luckily blocksize wars filtered out many people who believed that shills and moderators of centralized platforms would pick winning project for them. 

The power of bitcoin has always been the ability to survive despite the bad intentions of vested interests with all the money and power in the world. Bitcoin survives on its own logic, values and principles.

If you'd like to throw a couple coins towards a lone voice in the wilderness, and future where people can make a few sats and have a little community away from bucket shop criers, your satoshis will go a long way. 

Don't under-estimate the asymmetric power of your money.
